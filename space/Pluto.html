Welcome to Pluto!

Pluto was once considered the ninth planet from our sun, however within the last decade it has been reclassified as a dwarf planet.
The dwarf planet is the ninth-largest body that moves around the Sun.
Pluto is mainly made of rock and ice. It is quite small, it is about a fifth of the weight of the Earth's Moon.
Pluto's orbit around the sun is unusually sloped, this causes Pluto to sometimes go closer to the Sun than Neptune.
